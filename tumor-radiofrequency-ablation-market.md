<p><p>Tumor Radiofrequency Ablation (RFA) is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat various tumors by delivering heat generated from radiofrequency waves directly to the tumor tissue, leading to cell destruction. This technique is often utilized for tumors in the liver, kidneys, lungs, and other organs, offering an alternative to traditional surgical methods. The procedure is characterized by shorter recovery times, reduced hospital stays, and lower risk of complications, making it increasingly popular among patients and healthcare providers.</p><p>The Tumor Radiofrequency Ablation Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 9.3% during the forecast period, driven by advancements in RFA technology, increased cancer prevalence, and a growing preference for minimally invasive treatment options. Emerging trends include the integration of imaging technologies for enhanced precision in tumor targeting and the development of novel electrode designs to improve ablation efficacy. Additionally, increased awareness among healthcare professionals about the benefits of RFA and ongoing research for expanding its applications across different tumor types are contributing to market growth.
